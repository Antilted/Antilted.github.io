Q1: What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

My first impression is that there are a lot of color contrast along with a brief introduction of creator. I can tell that he is being very playful with it providing us with a lot of interactive features like the pixel movement and words rescrambling.

Q2: Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • I moved the mouse around and see a lot of colours and also it revealed that the highlighted words are swapped showing the authors feelings.
  • Second one I skipped through quickly because its just a triangle spinning and words scramble.
  • Third one is where I interacted the most because as I move the mouse around the pixel panel disperse creating a really appealing parallax, responsive effect.
  • At the bottom is just the author contact and bio with a similar effect to the second slide.

Q3: What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

The one with the pixel panel seems like appealing to me because I find the dynamics and mechanics of interacting with it really interesting. Also theres a word scrambling feature under it which seems unique also

Q4: What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

Basically, the entire portfolio is about you being interactive, what I mean by this is that we must move our mouse so that we could see what the elements reveal. I’ve also tried clicking on some of them but only the bio and contact pops up a new tab, from that I could see that the page is mostly a hover interaction.

Q5: What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

I feel like the author wants us to try to find the hidden objectives and results. The idea of making everything hover interactive keeps the simplicity and highlights the playfulness and color contrast of the author.

Q6: How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

Personally, I think that It shows what he can do but in a very simple way. It shows that you can be both minimal and still showing technical skills at the same time. Palat wants the viewers to unravel and interact with the elements he puts on, this I think is a fun way to get the audience to engage in his portfolio rather than just scrolling and clicking things.

Q7: What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

Personally, you need to take our time and hover your mouse cursor over the elements in the portfolio to reveal a hidden message or just to see scrambling movements. You should try to go at a slow pace since the web is short and more like a fun interactive show-reel.

Q8: How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The interactive experience teaches its use through visual concordances and dynamic feedback, elements shift, distort, and reassemble in response to user actions, revealing possible interactions over time. Rather than explicit instructions, it relies on gradual discovery as movement and transformation guide the user.
